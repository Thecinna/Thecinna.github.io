Astrostats

Primarily drawn from Modern Statistical Methods for Astronomy by Eric Feigelson and G. Babu

General summary

While I neither can nor am necessarily qualified to go into great detail as to the many statistical problems that appear in and require wrangling in order to be effective within the fields of modern physics and astronomy, I have created this page to serve as a handy (mostly personal) reference so that certain topics and problems explored in great detail in Feigelson and Babu's Modern Statistical Methods for Astronomy: With R Applications may be quickly indexed and personally preferred approaches may be noted. It should be noted that, for its price, the text serves as an excellent reference in its own right, though readers may be better served by more introductory works before stepping into it, as the authors, though certainly capable, created the work as a broad synopsis of methods and it largely assumes the reader already has at least some familiarity with statistical methods and theory.
