All good things must come to an end

Celebrating my time at Rice University and moving onto bigger and better things

Having started back at my undergraduate studies back in Spring of 2020 (promptly complicated by Covid and more), my time as an undergrad is quickly coming to an end. While details are sparse at this time, this page should serve to keep people updated on planning for the day of graduation and any other activities as they follow.

Pre-emptively answering some questions:

When is it?

Exactly what will be done and across which days it can be done will come down to a question of time (I expect to be tired), money (I am what we in the industry call "poor"), and the timing of events like commencement that are beyond my control. At the moment, it appears that commencement will take place Saturday evening, 10 May, 2025. At the moment, I expect to be busy much of the morning due to graduation-related errands. Similarly, while I expect commencement itself to run until a reasonable time, I presently have no strong plans for events to immediately follow. The next day would likely be best for any sort of larger activities or celebrations, with details to eventually be posted here. I understand this might be inconvenient for some who are coming in from out of town, but was thought of in part due to those coming in from several states away for whom it would be rude to attempt to condense the entirety of events into one day. I will not be offended if attendees are only able to be there for one event or the other and people are welcome to reach out to me if they have limited availability and want to be able to do something to take advantage of that, though I can't guarantee my own availability will be unlimited.

Where is it?

Per the above, many of the details are undetermined as of now. Commencement itself will take place at Rice Stadium in Houston, Texas, however; weather permitting.

What are you graduating with?

While things change all the time, I presently expect to receive a double BS in astrophysics and biophysics. It's possible, though not entirely likely, that I'll receive a minor as well; the most probable of the improbable bunch being religion. For a detailed breakdown of what all I've been studying, check my coursework summary.

What are you doing after graduation (and related activities)?

Ideally, sleep. Afterwards, I will likely be shifting my attention to relocating my life, as the graduate schools I'm applying to are located outside of Houston. While the list of schools I'm considering isn't something I'll make public at this time, they are something I might communicate privately. Ideally, by Fall of 2025 I will be relocated and beginning doctoral studies in either astrophysics, biophysics, or some applied variation thereof (engineering, applied physics, etc.).

But what about...?

There have existed a lot of ideas for what graduation would look like and while I might like to do in the time surrounding it. While these aren't cancelled, I don't want to go into them until everything can be properly planned and commited, rather than get everyone's hopes up.

Gifts?

I am terrible at giving gifts and thus expect none. I also tend to live such that there's not a lot that I need or want in my day to day. If you're committed to the idea of sending a graduation or any other sort of gift, consider contributing some sort of party supply, transportation help, or funding.

Formal invitations?

I am still in the process of drafting a list to mail formal invitations to. If you want one, please reach out with an address so I know where to send things.

I wish I could go, but...

Please reach out. While I am fairly broke, I do tend to know people who live in a wide variety of places that may be able to assist in transportation and will do what I can to help people myself. While I can't volunteer much in terms of sleeping space, I do have limited room available as well.
